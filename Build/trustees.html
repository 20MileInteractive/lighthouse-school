Jane Miller, Chair

Jane Miller has been a real estate broker in Nantucket for the past twenty years, currently with Atlantic East Nantucket Real Estate. Before entering real estate Jane taught French and Spanish at Norwich Free Academy in Norwich, Connecticut, and in the Nantucket Schools, where she started the AFS student exchange club. She continues to support AFS, believing strongly in the study abroad experience. She has tutored adult English language learners through the Literacy Project at the Nantucket Atheneum, served two terms on the Nantucket School Committee, and has served on the Town Association, an affiliate of the Civic League, for the past eight years, where she just began her second year as president of the organization. She loves reading, participating in the Atheneum World Voices Book Club, spending time with her five grandchildren (two of whom are students at Lighthouse School), and traveling with her husband, John.

D. Anne Atherton

A short pull quote from the person will go here to help convey their personality and philosopy. We will limit the characters to a certain amount so they display in a uniform manner.

D. Anne has been a community organizer for many years, both as a volunteer and a professional. Her commitment to children and education was evident in her work in her former community of Summit, New Jersey, which is also where she grew up. D. Anne was instrumental in planning, building and raising funds for a new, state-of-the-art, day-care facility in the mid-70s which was the center for a system of quality child-care services for families in Summit and surrounding communities. At the time, the Summit Child Care Center was a model for such comprehensive programs on the state, regional and national levels. She also served two terms on the Summit Board of Education, enabling her to give back to the school system that educated her. When the Atherton family moved to Nantucket to live year-round twenty years ago, D. Anne became involved in the Nantucket Public School System, advocating for increased parent involvement and advancing the organizational capacity of Friends of Nantucket Public Schools, our island's PTA. She also served on the founding steering committee of the Nantucket Education Trust and was a catalyst for the school-staff housing building project. In the mid-90s, D. Anne and Kim Horyn formed Atherton Horyn Associates (AHA), a consulting firm that provided services to Nantucket non-profits and businesses for several years, ranging from organizational development to community relations to fundraising to strategic planning. The first project for AHA was to work with a group of skating enthusiasts to make Nantucket Ice, their rink of dreams, a reality. AHA's subsequent clients represented the diversity of the community and included the Chamber of Commerce, Star of the Sea Youth Hostel, the Town of Nantucket, the MSPCA and the UMASS Boston Nantucket Field Station, to name a few. D. Anne and her husband, Rick, were recent Co-Presidents of the Nantucket Civic League, focusing on neighborhood and community issues, and D. Anne is presently a member of the Town's Roads and Right of Way Committee. On a personal note, one year ago, the Athertons became proud grandparents of a Nantucket native, making D. Anne's commitment to our island's children, their education and their future even stronger.

Caleb grew up on Nantucket and returned to the island after high school and college years spent at off island schools. This way of learning allows for a deeper knowledge of, and interest in, all subjects."

Lizbet Carroll Fuller

A short pull quote from the person will go here to help convey their personality and philosopy. We will limit the characters to a certain amount so they display in a uniform manner.

Lizbet co-founded Lighthouse School with Elizabeth Sundell in 1999 and has served as the Director of Education. She has worked with young children for over 25 years in pre-school and elementary school classrooms. She moved to Nantucket in 1991 to teach at NES before leaving and creating her own pre-school program, which evolved into the current Lighthouse Small School. Lizbet's philosophy of education is inspired and informed by the work of Rudolf Steiner (founder of Waldorf Education), the Integrated Day approach of the Antioch New England Graduate School, the social curriculum and philosophy of the Mead School, and her practical experience in the classroom. Lizbet currently serves as an educational advisor to Lighthouse faculty. She is married to Waterman Chris Fuller and son Eli happily attends Lighthouse School. "We set out to create a school that honors the magic of childhood while creatively introducing children to the ideas, information, and tools they will need to construct meaningful and rewarding lives. In turn, the school has generated a wonderful and caring community of teachers, staff members, families, and friends."

Kate Andrews

An island native, Kate grew up in and around Boston before earning her B.A in English and Art History at Lafayette College. She studied Interior Design at Parsons School of Design and Rhode Island School of Design and then returned to Nantucket to build upon these studies. Solo travel to Indonesia, Australia and New Zealand provided a timely shift to a new field of work. Kate felt right at home when first walking down the halls of Lighthouse School and shortly thereafter joined Erin Dancik's Primary class as a teaching assistant. She undertook her own Small School Class the following year and has moved up with her first students to teach Kinderclass, Lower Primary and Middle Primary. In 2010, Kate received her M.Ed. from Fitchburg State College. Five years ago, Kate founded her own small Summer Camp, which is in full swing in July and August. In her spare time, she enjoys oil painting, strolling through the woods with her dog and practicing yoga.

J. Brent Tartamella, Treasurer

A short pull quote from the person will go here to help convey their personality and philosopy. We will limit the characters to a certain amount so they display in a uniform manner.

The Westmoor Club's General Manager, J. Brent Tartamella, CCM, CCE, joined Westmoor in 2004 as the Club's founding General Manager. Originally from Ohio, Brent attended UMASS Amherst and Oxford Brooks University in England. In 2009, he was awarded the Club Manager of the Year and received the Distinguished Managers Award from Boardroom Magazine. In 2011, he was named President of the New England Club Manager's Association of America, a professional Association for managers of membership clubs, with close to 7,000 members. Brent has had the opportunity to watch the dynamic process of growth and maturity of the Club while assisting the members and staff and improving the Club's operations. Most importantly, he aims to protect Westmoor's family and health-oriented culture that celebrates great sport and an active well-lived life. Brent's four-year-old son Ryder has recently completed his first year at NLS, and along with Ryder's excitement, Brent looks forward to the growth and development of the school.
